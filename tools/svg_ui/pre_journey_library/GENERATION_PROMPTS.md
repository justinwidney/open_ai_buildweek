# Pre-journey generation prompt set

Mode: OpenAI built-in image generation, one generation call per scene, using
the three references in `../route_tile_library/PRE_JOURNEY_IMAGEGEN_PROMPT.md`.

## Shared direction

Create a new, wide fantasy game environment in the same polished hand-painted
watercolor and gouache language as the references. This is an earlier point in
the life journey, before the viewer steps onto a formal cream road. Use a
grounded forest or clearing viewpoint with moss, roots, ferns, stones, and
wildflowers in the unmarked foreground. Put subtle natural choices in the
middle distance. Show floating highlands, turquoise cloud-filled water,
waterfalls, mist, and exactly one blue-roofed castle on the upper-center
horizon. The castle must be extremely small—about 2–5% of image width. Keep a
quiet left-middle region for translucent onboarding UI and retain the castle in
the center mobile crop.

Constraints: no person, formal route beneath the viewer, crossroads directly
under the camera, signs, arrows, symbols, numbers, text, UI, logos, watermark,
frame, modern objects, danger, photorealism, giant castle, aerial map, or
duplicated buildings.

## Scene 01 — Forest threshold

The viewer stands inside an old pine forest. Two faint natural foot-trails
become legible only in the middle distance before disappearing among the trees.
Use hopeful morning beams, cool mist, and a sense of childhood wonder.

## Scene 02 — Sunlit clearing

The viewer has emerged into an open highland meadow. Across the clearing, imply
three possibilities: a narrow trail into birches, an open grassy central rise,
and a soft path curving right beside rocks. Use fresh dawn light after rain and
a mood of curiosity and early independence.

## Scene 03 — Streamside choice

The viewer is in a sheltered woodland glade beside a shallow clear stream.
Across the middle third, stepping stones lead toward a sunlit grove while a
natural log crossing and faint bank trail suggest another direction. Use
tranquil gold light and cool blue mist; the stream is not a road.

## Scene 04 — Two-way dirt-path fork

Match the sunlit clearing closely. One warm tan dirt path enters from the
bottom center and forms a simple, unmistakable Y fork in the middle third. Its
two equal branches remain continuous toward opposite forest edges. There is no
straight center branch after the fork.

## Scene 05 — Path exits left

One warm tan dirt path enters from the bottom center, follows a broad natural
S-bend through the clearing, and physically crosses the left canvas edge in the
middle-lower third. There is no fork, right branch, or center continuation.

## Scene 06 — Path exits right

One warm tan dirt path enters from the bottom center, follows a broad natural
S-bend through the clearing, and physically crosses the right canvas edge in
the middle-lower third. There is no fork, left branch, or center continuation.

## Scene 07 — Left / straight / right

One foreground path divides at one clear junction into exactly three outgoing
paths of similar importance: one curves left, one continues straight through
the center meadow, and one curves right. Broad green wedges separate all three
branches; there is no fourth route.

## Scene 08 — Far-left / near-left / straight

One foreground path divides into an asymmetric three-route fan: a far-left
branch crosses the left edge, a gentler near-left branch enters the birch grove,
and a straight branch continues through the center. Keep the right meadow
path-free so the two different leftward decisions remain unambiguous.

## Scene 09 — Staged left / straight / right

Use two spatially separated Y-forks. At a low, near junction, a left route
peels away while the trunk continues. After a long uninterrupted section, a
second junction much higher in the frame divides that trunk into straight and
right routes. The network has exactly three final destinations, but never one
trident junction.
